  • Commonsense Knowledge Aware Conversation Generation with Graph Attention IJCAI-2018

Background

  • 以往的方法大多数都是从语义方面进行考虑，如何利用上下文的语义信息从而使得对话的回复质量（通常分为相关性，多样性）更高，这一方面目前已经取得了不错的成就，不管是基于检索还是基于生成。尽管如此还是会出现回复缺乏逻辑性（一致性问题），回复太通用（多样性问题）。这就使得对话系统利用外部知识成为必要，因为很多上下文实体的相关知识是不存在于训练语料中的。
  • 目前已经有一些工作做了对于外部知识的探索。知识按结构分为有结构的知识（知识图谱）和无结构的知识（餐厅的评论，手册），有结构的知识因为其大规模和通用性常用于开放领域的对话，而无结构的知识常用于特定领域的对话，帮助完成特定任务。

Augmenting End-to-End Dialogue Systems with Commonsense Knowledge

Motivation

验证在对话中引入Commonsense Knowledge的有效性。

在基于检索的对话系统中引入：

  • 容易验证
  • 少量训练数据即可

Model

  • Dual-LSTM

以Dual-LSTM为基本架构，也就是最早的Ubuntu数据集上的模型为基本架构：

\(f(x,y) = \sigma (x^TWy)\)

一个双仿射来计算上下文和回复之间的匹配得分

  • Commonsense Knowledge Retrieval

  • 定义：对于每一个concept，与他相关的关系三元组为assertion\(<c_1,r,c_2>\)

  • step1：预先构建好一个字典，key为每一个concept，值为与concept相关的所有assertions

  • step2：对于每一个上下文，检索与之匹配的少于五个的concept的所有assertions

  • Tri-LSTM Encoder

  • 将assertion转换成序列：\([c_{11},c_{12},c_{13},...,r,c_{21},c_{22},c_{23},...]\)

  • 用额外的LSTM对assertions进行建模，分别与候选回复做匹配，对得到的所有的匹配得分做最大化

  • 最后将最大的匹配得分与Dual-LSTM的匹配得分相加

A Knowledge-Grounded Neural Conversation Model

Motivation

这篇文章作者认为在大规模对话语料中训练的神经网络模型可以生产通用的回复但却缺乏内容性，而目标导向的对话系统却又有明确的槽需要填充而缺乏通用性，所以这篇文章的目的是想在通用对话模型的基础上引入外部知识，是的对话系统的回复既有通用性又有内容性。大量的对话数据用来学习对话模式，外部知识用来拓展实体。

  • previous:

  • models have been mostly applied to casual scenarios

  • lack grounding in the real world and do not have access to external knowledge (textual or structured)

  • ours:

  • allowing the model to be versatile and applicable: conditioning responses on both conversation history and external “facts”
  • fully data-driven models: versatility and scalability
  • produce models that are usefully grounded in external knowledge

  • Much of this information is not found in structured databases either, but is textual, and can be mined from online resources

Our purpose is to explore the space of less clearly goal- directed, but nonetheless informative conversation that does not demand explicit slot-filling.

  • dialogue datasets are still very far from containing discussions of every entry in Wikipedia, Foursquare, Goodreads, or IMDB

  • many conversational patterns exhibited in the data (e.g., for similar entities) would be redundant

Model

  • 准备阶段
  • 构建外部知识（事实），取无解构文本中的实体词，以命名实体为索引
  • 取对话上下文中焦点词构成query
  • 用query去外部知识中检索所有相关的事实

得到所有上下文的相关事实，将上下文和检索到的事实分别编码

  • multi-task learning

  • one purely conversational \((S,R)\)

  • other task exposes the full model \(({f_1, ...,f_k,S}, R)\)

  • Dialog Encoder and Decoder（GRU）

two-layer

  • Facts Encoder

\(S = {s_1, s_2, ..., s_n}\) , \(F = {f_1, f_2, ..., f_n}\)

S: input sentence ---> RNN encode ----> u

F: bag of words \(r_i\)

​

Memory Network (1-layer)

Experiments

DataSet

  • Foursquare:
  • comments left by customers about restaurants and other, usually commercial, establishments.
  • 1.1M tips relating to establishments in North America
  • Twitter:
  • 23M general dataset of 3-turn conversations
  • 1M grounded dataset: on the basis of Twitter handles found in the Foursquare tip data, collected approximately 1 million two-turn conversations that contain entities that tie to Foursquare

Grounded Conversation Datasets

  • calculates cosine similarity between the input sentence and each of the tips and retains 10 tips with the highest score.

  • dev and test(4K):

  • Perplexity according to a 1-gram LM trained on all the tips containing that handle

  • χ-square score, which measures how much content each token bears in relation to the handle.

    15k top-ranked conversations using the LM score and 15k using the chi-square score. A further 15k conversations were randomly sampled. then randomly sampled 10k conversations from these 45K.

    Crowdsourced human judges:4K

Multi-Task Learning

  • FACTS task: full model \(({f_1, ...,f_k,S}, R)\)
  • NOFACTS task: \((S,R)\)
  • AUTOENCODER task: \(({f_1, ...,f_k,S}, f_i)\) replace the response with each of the facts

AUTOENCODER tasks helps inject more factual content into the response.

  • SEQ2SEQ: Trained on task NOFACTS with the 23M general conversation dataset.
  • MTASK: Trained on two instances of the NOFACTS task, respectively with the 23M general dataset and 1M grounded dataset (but without the facts)
  • MTASK-R: Trained on the NOFACTS task with the 23M dataset, and the FACTS task with the 1M grounded dataset.
  • MTASK-F: Trained on the NOFACTS task with the 23M dataset, and the AUTOENCODER task with the 1M dataset.
  • MTASK-RF: Blends MTASK-F and MTASK-R, as it incorporates 3 tasks: NOFACTS with the 23M general dataset, FACTS with the 1M grounded dataset, and AUTOENCODER again with the 1M dataset.

Decoding and Reranking

  • Decoder:

beam-search decoder: 200

  • Reranking:

reranking score: \(logP(R|S,F) + \lambda logP(S|R) + \gamma |R|\)

  • \(logP(R|S,F)\): decoder
  • \(\gamma |R|\): word count
  • \(\lambda logP(S|R)\): deal with generating commonplace and generic responses (lijiwei那一套)

Evaluation Metrics

corpus-level BLEU，perplexity and lexical diversity

Commonsense Knowledge Aware Conversation Generation with Graph Attention

大家好，这是我本周讲的论文，本篇论文是发表在IJCAI2018上的一篇Distinguished Paper，本文作者在对话生成的任务上尝试使用大规模的常识知识并验证了其有效性，同时作者提出了基于图的动态和静态attention机制将知识实体关系图作为整体进行建模。

Motivation

  • previous：
  • highly dependent on the quality of unstructured texts
  • limited by the small-scale, domain-specific knowledge
  • make use of knowledge triples (entities) separately and independently, instead of treating knowledge triples as a whole in a graph
  • demonstrate how large-scale commonsense knowledge can facilitate language understanding and generation（first attempt that uses large-scale commonsense knowledge in conversation generation）
  • our model treats each knowledge graph as a whole, which encodes more structured, connected semantic information in the graphs

Model

  • augments the semantic information of the post （编码阶段）
  • the model attentively reads the retrieved knowledge graphs and the knowledge triples（解码阶段）

Task Definition

  • Each word corresponds to a graph
  • Each graph consists of a set of triples
  • Each triple (head entity, relation, tail entity)
  • TransE to represent the entities and relations: adopt a MLP to bridge the representation gap between knowledge base and unstructured conversational texts, a knowledge triple \(τ\) is represented by \(k = (h, r, t) = MLP(TransE(h, r, t))\)
